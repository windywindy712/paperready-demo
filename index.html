Highlights

  • AX-114 shows blue-selective activation at the single-channel level
  • Light responses broaden from channel to retina to cortex across wavelengths
  • Unilateral intravitreal AAV gives mostly local retinal exposure in primates
  • A single intravitreal dose was well tolerated over 48 weeks in advanced retinal degeneration

Summary

Optogenetic therapy can render surviving inner retinal neurons light-sensitive after photoreceptor loss. Here we characterize AX-114, an engineered high-sensitivity channelrhodopsin delivered by intravitreal AAV (AX-114). In a first-in-human, randomized, single-masked, sham-controlled trial (NCT0XXXXXXX) in ten adults with advanced inherited retinal degeneration, a single intravitreal dose of AX-114 was well tolerated over 48 weeks, with no serious adverse events. Exploratory functional changes were compatible with biological activity but not definitive evidence of efficacy. These findings define a translational framework for device-free optogenetic vision restoration and motivate confirmatory trials.

Introduction

In preclinical models, intravitreal AAV delivery produced robust expression and photocurrent activation at luminance levels comparable to moonlight, indicating that AX-114 can activate retinal circuits under very dim light without light-amplifying devices.

Results

Visual Acuity Outcomes

Mean change from baseline in BCVA was 0.55±0.30 logMAR in study eyes and 0.58±0.34 logMAR in fellow eyes (n=8). Fellow-eye change was comparable to, and on average slightly larger than, study-eye change. Because changes of similar magnitude occurred in eyes that received no injection, these BCVA changes cannot be attributed specifically to the treated study eye, and are reported as exploratory observations.

Chromatic Response

At Week 52, treated participants identified and distinguished chromatic stimuli above chance. Performance above chance does not by itself establish trichromatic color perception, and the underlying mechanism is not directly demonstrated by these data.
